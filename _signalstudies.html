Chart legend

What each line means.

Signal Lab charts overlay five energy lines on top of price. Each one measures a different force acting on the stock. Together, they reveal whether a narrative is building, sustaining, or about to break.

Price
The actual stock price over time. This is what every other tool shows you. The energy lines tell you why it's moving — and when it will stop.
Narrative Energy (Blue)
How much story volume exists around this ticker. High blue means the media is talking. It doesn't mean they're right — just that the narrative is loud.
Physics Energy (Yellow)
The real energy behind the story — derived from verified claims, source credibility, and SEC alignment. When yellow is high, the narrative has substance. When it's low, the story is running on fumes.
Temporal Energy (Teal) — The Predictive Signal
This is the core discovery. Temporal Energy models how long a narrative can sustain price. When it collapses, price follows. When it surges from a low base, upside is building. This is the line that makes the system predictive.
Narrative Pressure (Violet)
Measures coordination and herding — when multiple sources push the same thesis simultaneously. High pressure means the narrative is fragile, dependent on consensus that could break at any moment.
Sentiment Dots (Green / Red)
Individual bullish (green) and bearish (red) sentiment events plotted on the chart. Clusters of one color reveal where consensus is forming — and where it might be wrong.
What to watch for

Four patterns that predict price moves.

You don't need to memorize formulas. These four visual patterns appear on the chart before major price moves. Learn to spot them and you'll see what the system sees.

Bearish
Peak Narrative (Saturation)
Blue and yellow both maxed at 100. The story is fully saturated — everyone who's going to buy has bought. There's nowhere to go but down. This is the strongest short signal in the system.
Warning
Hollow Narrative
Blue is high but yellow is low. The story is loud but empty — media volume without substance. The narrative is running on fumes. When the story can't sustain itself, price corrects.
Critical
Temporal Energy Collapse
The teal line drops sharply toward zero. This means the narrative's ability to sustain price is exhausting. Historically, stocks drop an average of 9% when Temporal Energy collapses below the critical threshold.
Bullish
Energy Rebuilding
Temporal Energy surging from a low base while blue energy is low. New energy is entering while old narratives decay. This is the rarest signal — and historically precedes an average 9% gain.
