**Post:** "Reddit's Data Moat: Why RDDT's Position in the AI Training Pipeline Has Structural Durability"

## Errors Corrected

### 1. $203M licensing revenue figure (CRITICAL)
- **Original claim:** "$203 million in full-year 2024 revenue" from the data licensing segment
- **Correction:** The $203M was the total aggregate contract value of licensing arrangements entered in January 2024, spanning two to three years — not a single year's revenue. Reddit expected to recognize a minimum of $66.4M of that amount as revenue during 2024, with the remainder in subsequent years. The original article inflated the licensing revenue figure by approximately 3x.
- **Sources:** TechCrunch, BestofAI reporting on Reddit's S-1/10-K disclosures

### 2. Licensing segment disclosure timeline
- **Original claim:** "Reddit's data licensing segment, disclosed separately beginning in Q3 2024"
- **Correction:** Licensing revenue first appeared in Q2 2024 results under "other revenue," totaling $28.1M that quarter. Q3 2024 data licensing came in at $33.2M. For full-year 2024, advertising made up ~91% of total revenue — licensing was a much smaller slice than originally implied.
- **Sources:** CNBC (Q2 results), Yahoo Finance (Q3 results), Statista (full-year revenue mix)
