Why this matters

It's not an LLM wrapper.

The AI-stock-tip space has filled up with thin shells around black boxes — paste a ticker, get a confident-sounding paragraph that may or may not match the actual 10-K. Market Prism is built the other direction.

The language model is one tool inside a seven-step forensic pipeline. Every narrative claim is grounded in primary-source documents — filings, earnings transcripts and company guidance — then scored across drift, VMS, decay and mass, cross-checked for coordination across outlets, and classified for exhaustion and hype-bubble traps. If the model wants to say something the source doesn't support, the pipeline catches it before it reaches your screen.

Three patents pending. The model is a tool. The forensic chain is the product.

Benefits

What you actually get out of using it.

Less time reading content-farm "analysis." More time looking at evidence. A daily slate you can audit, a forensic page per ticker, and a heatmap that shows the pressure before the move.

Edge

Front-run narrative exhaustion.

Saturation flagging tells you when a story has run out of room — historically the moment the price reverses. Most tools tell you the story exists; we tell you when it's over.

Trust

Every claim has a citation.

If the dashboard says a company is "guiding lower," you can click through to the exact line in the 8-K. No paraphrase, no GPT confabulation.

Speed

One screen, the whole forensic read.

Scorecard, source alignment, source breakdown, coordination flag, price series — all in one ticker page. No five-tab Bloomberg dance.

Accountability

Public hit/miss tracking.

$1.5M paper book, every Daily Play tagged with hit_date and alpha-vs-SPY. If our slate is bleeding, you'll see it before we do.

Filter

Domain authority over volume.

A messy post from a known industry voice outranks a thousand polished AI-generated articles. We score the source, not the word count.

Defense

Coordination flags catch campaigns.

Five outlets publishing near-identical framing inside an hour isn't consensus — it's a campaign. We surface it before you trade into it.
